1) Euphoria: Many drugs, especially stimulants and some opioids, can induce feelings of 
intense pleasure and euphoria.

2) Mood Changes: Drugs can cause shifts in mood, leading to feelings of happiness, sadness, 
or anxiety.

3) Anxiety and Paranoia: Some drugs, particularly stimulants and hallucinogens, can trigger 
or exacerbate feelings of anxiety and paranoia.

4) Confusion and Disorientation: Certain drugs can impair cognitive function, leading to 
confusion and difficulty concentrating.

5) Impaired Judgment: Drug use can lead to impaired decision-making and risky behaviors.

6) Memory Impairment: Some drugs can lead to short-term memory loss or difficulty forming 
new memories.

7) Depression: Substance abuse can contribute to or worsen symptoms of depression.

8) Psychosis: In some cases, drug use, especially heavy use of hallucinogens or stimulants, can lead to symptoms of psychosis, 
including delusions and hallucinations.
