1) Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS) and Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN): These 
   are severe skin reactions that can be triggered by certain medications, 
   including antibiotics, anticonvulsants, and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory 
   drugs (NSAIDs).
            
2) Drug-Induced Lier Injury: Some medications, likeacetaminophen, certain 
   antibiotics, and antifungal drugs, can cause liver damage or hepatitis.
                    
3) Drug-Induced Cardiomyopathy: Certain drugs, such as some chemotherapy agents 
   and certain antipsychotic medications, can lead to weakening of the heart 
   muscle and heart-related issues.
                    
4) Nephrotoxicity: Certain medications, including certain antibiotics, NSAIDs, 
   and some antiviral drugs, can cause kidney damage or impair kidney function.
                    
5) Drug-Induced Pulmonary Toxicity: Some drugs, like certain chemotherapy agents 
   and certain antibiotics,can lead to lung problems or even pulmonary fibrosis.
                    
6) Agranulocytosis: Some medications, like certain antipsychotic drugs and 
   certain antithyroid drugs, can cause a severe drop in white blood cell 
   count, leading to an increased risk of infections.
                    
7) Serotonin Syndrome: This can be caused by interactions between medications thatincrease serotonin levels in 
   the brain, leading to symptoms like agitation, high body temperature, rapid heart rate, and more.
                    
8) Tardive Dyskinesia: This is a neurological disorder caused by long-term use of certain antipsychotic 
   medications, resulting in involuntary repetitive movements.
